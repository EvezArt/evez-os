RE: Historical Records Request — Blyth Family, 1001 Sage St, TB Hospitals, Sanborn Maps, Chinese Community, Masonic Lodge, Underground Tunnels

Dear Staff,

I am writing to request access to and copies of historical records and materials in your collection related to several topics concerning Evanston and Uinta County history. I am a former resident of 1001 Sage St, Evanston, and I am researching the history of this property and its surrounding area.

Specifically, I request the following:

1. **Blyth Family Records**
   - Any information, photographs, documents, or archival materials pertaining to the Blyth (or Blythe) family of Evanston, including but not limited to the founders and operators of the Blyth & Fargo store (established circa 1872)
   - Family member names, dates, residential addresses, business affiliations, and biographical materials
   - Any records of Masonic affiliations or memberships for members of the Blyth family
   - Any obituaries, funeral records, or burial site information for the Blyth family

2. **1001 Sage Street — Photographs and Building Records**
   - Any photographs, drawings, or depictions of buildings located at or near 1001 Sage St, Evanston, WY 82930
   - Any historical documentation of the construction, use, or ownership of this property

3. **Tuberculosis Hospitals / Sanatoriums**
   - Any records, photographs, or historical documentation regarding tuberculosis hospitals, sanatoriums, or treatment facilities in or near Evanston, Uinta County
   - Any information about facilities that may have operated in residential or converted buildings

4. **Sanborn Fire Insurance Maps**
   - Any Sanborn fire insurance maps for the city of Evanston that show the area of Sage Street between 9th Street and 11th Street, for all available years of publication
   - Any other historic plat maps or atlases depicting this area

5. **Chinese Immigrant Community Records**
   - Any records, photographs, or documentation of the Chinese immigrant community in Evanston and the surrounding area (including Almy)
   - Burial locations, cemetery records, and any information regarding Chinese burial grounds or remains
   - Any records of the Chinese exclusion era as it affected the Evanston community

6. **Masonic Lodge Records**
   - Any records or information regarding Masonic Lodge(s) in Evanston — charter dates, membership rolls, meeting locations
   - Specifically, any records of members who achieved the 33° of the Scottish Rite bearing the surname Blyth or Blythe
   - Any photographs, directories, or ephemera related to Masonic activity in Evanston

7. **Underground Tunnel Maps**
   - Any maps, photographs, or documentation of underground tunnels in downtown Evanston
   - Any historical accounts or engineering records of subterranean passages, whether for utility, railroad, commercial, or other purposes

I am happy to pay reasonable reproduction and research fees. If any of these materials are available digitally, I would greatly appreciate electronic copies. Please contact me to arrange access or to advise on any fees associated with this request.

Thank you for your dedication to preserving Evanston's history. I look forward to hearing from you.
